Abstract

QR8 studies a compact mixed-precision route to QR factorization: an INT8-oriented initialization is used to expose a cheap triangular structure, while FP64 refinement restores numerical reliability.

The aim is not to replace stable dense QR methods in all regimes. The aim is narrower: to examine when a low-precision starting point can be made useful by a carefully placed high-precision correction.

\( A \approx QR \), with orthogonality repaired by refinement.

Method

QR8 follows the Cholesky-QR idea: form a Gram matrix, factor it, and obtain the orthogonal factor through a triangular solve. The distinctive point is the use of a low-precision initializer before refinement.

In practical terms, QR8 treats the low-precision stage as a scaffold. The final judgment is made in higher precision, where loss of orthogonality and residual error can be measured.

Principle

Use cheap arithmetic to approach the structure; use careful arithmetic to correct it.

Target

A small, inspectable experiment for mixed-precision QR and conditioning-sensitive refinement. 

Given a matrix A

1. Build a low-precision representation of A.
2. Form an approximate Gram structure.
3. Compute a triangular Cholesky factor R.
4. Solve for a provisional Q.
5. Recheck residual and orthogonality in FP64.
6. Refine when the conditioning permits it.

Report:
  ||A - Q R||
  ||I - Q^T Q||
  condition estimate
  precision path

Conditioning note

Cholesky-QR is sensitive to conditioning because the Gram matrix squares the condition number. The control below is only a visual note: it shows why refinement becomes more important as the matrix becomes harder.
